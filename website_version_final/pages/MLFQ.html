Multilevel Feedback Queue

What is it? Approaches, and Rules

A multilevel feedback queue is an approach to scheduling. It has multiple distinct queues and is assigned to different priority levels. It uses priority to decide which job runs.

  1. It uses priorities to decide which job should run at a given time a job with higher priority (i.e., a job on a higher queue) is chosen to run.
  2. More than one job may be on a given queue (that have the same priority), it uses round-robin scheduling among those jobs to alternate time slices between jobs of the same priority.
  3. It ensures that every job gets some CPU time, and allows MLFQ to determine the optimal priority of each job.
  4. Each job is allotted an amount of time it can spend at the highest priority - the top of the queue. It ensures that one job cannot stay at the highest priority forever, and helps free up the top queue for the highest priority processes, setting up for rule #5
  5. After some time period, all the jobs are moved in the system to the top most queue.

According to the 5 rules, time slices are used to execute a job. There are primarily 5 kinds of scheduling approaches:

  • First Come First Serve (FCFS)
  • Shortest-Job-First (SJF)
  • Shortest Remaining Time
  • Priority Scheduling
  • Round Robin
